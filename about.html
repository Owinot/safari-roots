Who We Are

Our Story

The Beginning

From a Family Kitchen
to a Nairobi Institution

Safari Roots was born in 2012 in the heart of Nairobi's Westlands neighbourhood, the brainchild of Chef Amara Ochieng and her co-founder, Kwame Nkrumah. Both had grown up in homes where the kitchen was the centre of the universe — where grandmothers whispered secrets into iron pots, and the aroma of spices told you everything was going to be okay.

After years of cooking in hotels and restaurants across East and West Africa, Amara and Kwame wanted something more: a space where African food could be celebrated in all its diversity, not reduced to a single stereotype.

Today, Safari Roots draws recipes and inspiration from over ten African nations, constantly evolving while remaining rooted in the traditions that gave each dish its soul.

"We cook with the hands of our grandmothers and the hearts of our customers."
— Chef Amara Ochieng

Why We Exist

Our Mission & Values

Celebrate Africa

We believe African cuisine is one of the world's great culinary traditions. Every menu choice we make is a vote of confidence in that belief.

Community First

Over 80% of our ingredients are sourced from small-scale Kenyan farmers and artisanal producers. When you eat here, you support an entire ecosystem.

Uncompromising Quality

We grind our own spices daily. We bake our own bread. We marinate overnight. Good food cannot be rushed, and we refuse to cut corners.

Sustainable Choices

From our biodegradable packaging to our composting programme, we're committed to leaving the land in better shape than we found it.

The People

Meet Our Team

Amara Ochieng

Executive Chef & Co-Founder

Trained in Accra, Lagos, and Paris, Amara blends classical technique with deep West African roots. She oversees every dish that leaves the kitchen.

Kwame Nkrumah

Restaurateur & Co-Founder

A hospitality veteran with 20 years across Nairobi, Lagos and Cape Town, Kwame ensures every guest leaves with a story worth telling.

Fatima Al-Hassan

Head Pastry Chef

Born in Khartoum and raised on North African sweets, Fatima brings the continent's dessert traditions to life in every baklava, pudding, and mandazi.

Sipho Dlamini

Head of Beverage

Cape Town native and self-described "fermentation philosopher," Sipho curates our drinks programme with a focus on African botanicals and local craft beverages.
